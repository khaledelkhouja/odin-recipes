KAAK WARKA

KAAK WARKA RECIPE

Kaak Warka is a sweet cookie made of a light layer of butter enhanced dough and made of almond flour. It is composed of marzipan enhanced geranium and rose liquid. It appears like an engagement ring and is sometimes included with gorgeous flowers.

This fine Tunisian sweet is common during the holy month of Ramadan, wedding celebrations and even during everyday tea time, it is also popular in Algeria and other Maghreb regions.

Preparation of the dough

Ingredients

  • 250 gr of special kaak warka flour
  • 125 g of rancid butter
  • 1/5 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon powdered white food coloring
  • A pinch of salt
  • 80 ml of hot water

Preparation steps

  1. Sift the flour, baking powder, salt and food coloring into a bowl
  2. Add the cooled rancid butter and work the mixture well
  3. Gradually pour the amount of hot water while continuing to knead with your hands for a few minutes to obtain a soft, elastic and very smooth dough.
  4. Form a ball and let the dough rest for at least two hours or more in the refrigerator

Preparation of the stuffing

Ingredients

  • 250 g almond powder
  • 125 g icing sugar
  • Rosehip flower water or rose water or geranium water

Preparation steps

  1. In another container, pour the almond powder, icing sugar and rosehip flower water
  2. Mix all the ingredients without kneading to obtain a non-sticky dough
  3. Make sausages 7cm long by 1cm in diameter then reserve them in a freezer bag

Preparation of Kaak Warka

  1. Roll out the dough thinly using a rolling pin on a work surface. The dough should be thin and transparent.
  2. Cut out rectangles using a 7.5 cm template.
  3. Place a sausage of marzipan at the beginning of the sheet then roll the dough 2 to 3 times on itself.
  4. Cut the sausage formed and weld the two ends of the sausage using rancid butter or rosehip flower water.
  5. Smooth the edges well in order to obtain pretty Kaak Warka.
  6. Arrange the Kaak Warka on a baking sheet lined with baking paper.
  7. Bake the cakes over low heat at 150Â°C max (non-circulating heat) for 20 minutes.
    • the cooking by looking at the underside of the Kaak Warka which should not brown but just have a little golden color
  8. Let them cool completely before moving them to an airtight container to keep them fresh.
